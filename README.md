## Likely available at a US public library

These are popular enough or from major publishers that most decent-sized public library systems should have them (or can interlibrary-loan them easily):

- Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus — Wittgenstein is a classic, widely held
- Side Hustle — Chris Guillebeau is a bestselling author, very common
- Cinematography: Theory and Practice — Blain Brown, popular film textbook, often stocked
- The Cambridge Illustrated History of China — Cambridge "Illustrated History" series is widely held
- Empires in World History — Burbank & Cooper, well-known and from Princeton UP
- Critical Thinking — William Hughes, common intro logic/critical thinking text
- Queen Victoria and Her Prime Ministers — Anne Somerset, popular history from a trade publisher
- Ultimate Presentations — Jay Surti, popular press (Kogan Page)
- Good Arguments — Missimer, fairly common logic text

## Harder to find — more likely at a university library

These are more specialized, academic, or niche enough that a public library is less likely to carry them:

- Impacts of Modernities — Lamarre & Kang, academic volume, fairly niche
- Early China/Ancient Greece — Shankman & Durrant, comparative studies monograph, very specialized
- Ancient Greek Gadgets and Machines — Brumbaugh, older academic title (1966), uncommon
- Philosophy and Probability — Childers, Oxford UP but niche topic
- Film Lighting — Malkiewicz, specialized film craft book
- Critical Reading and Writing for Postgraduates — Wallace & Wray, aimed at grad students, more common in UK/academic collections
- First Philosophy — Andrew Bailey, a Canadian philosophy anthology/textbook, mostly held by universities
- The Education of a Photographer — Traub et al., niche art/photography anthology
- Pre-Modern East Asia: To 1800 — Ebrey et al., university textbook
- A History of East Asia — Holcombe, university textbook
- Composition — David Prakel (AVA/Basics Creative Photography series), more specialized
